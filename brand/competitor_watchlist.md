# Competitor Watchlist — Thailand Ride-Hailing

Market context: Thailand ride-hailing ≈ USD 2.6B (2025) → 4.38B (2031),
Bangkok holds ~55%. 11 DLT-licensed apps. Tada = emerging challenger.

## Grab (dominant leader)
- Commission ~25–30%. Multi-service super-app.
- Plays: big influencer + government partnerships (TAT "Amazing Thailand,
  Travel with Grab", promo codes like AMAZING25).
- Watch: https://www.grab.com/th/en/press/ and https://www.grab.com/th/blog/
- Play package id: com.grabtaxi.passenger
- Counter-angle: commission burden on drivers; impersonal scale.

## LINE MAN Wongnai (#2, most direct driver-side threat — only ~10% commission)
- Plays: "Everyday Lowest Prices" (THB 300M), ACTTention strategy
  (Attract → Connect → Trigger), 300k+ monthly stackable deals.
- Watch: https://lmwn.com/ and LINE MAN Facebook page.
- Play package id: com.linecorp.linemanth (driver app: com.linecorp.lineman.driver)
- Counter-angle: 10% is still not 0%; quantify the gap at real income levels.

## Bolt (growing challenger)
- Budget positioning, price promos. Play package id: ee.mtakso.client
- Counter-angle: cheap for riders ≠ fair for drivers.

## inDrive (niche)
- Fare-negotiation model, driver-friendly messaging — closest narrative rival.
- Play package id: sinet.startup.inDriver
- Counter-angle: negotiation friction vs Tada's simple flat THB 20 fee.

## Nine signal types to monitor weekly
launches, pricing, messaging shifts, new features, hiring signals,
customer wins, promotional cadence, app-review sentiment, driver grievances
(Pantip + Facebook driver groups — search, don't scrape logged-in content).

## Rules
Public data only: press pages, blogs, Facebook Ads Library, app store
reviews, coupon aggregators. Never scrape logged-in dashboards or any
driver/rider personal data.
